Privacy Policy & Terms of Use

Last updated: July 12, 2026

Can You Go 82-0? is a free, fan-made basketball simulator. It doesn't require an account, a sign-up, or any payment. This page explains what little data the game does handle, and the basic terms for using it.

Information we collect

  • Anonymous gameplay analytics — the game logs anonymous events (e.g. a season being started or simulated, win/loss totals, which coach or era was chosen) via Google Firebase Analytics. This isn't tied to your name or identity.
  • Optional Global Leaderboard submissions — if you choose to submit a run to the public Global Leaderboard, the team name you type, your record, chosen coach, era, chemistry score, and starting five are stored and shown publicly to every visitor. This only happens if you press "Submit" — don't type anything in the team name field you wouldn't want public. Global Leaderboard entries aren't linked to any account and can't be edited or deleted by us on request, since they're anonymous by design.
  • Locally saved progress — your personal best record, Trophy Room, and collected Legends are saved either in your browser (localStorage) or, if you're playing through CrazyGames, via CrazyGames' own Data Module tied to your CrazyGames account if you have one. This data stays on your device or with CrazyGames — it isn't sent to us.

Third-party services

  • Google Firebase (Analytics & the Firestore database behind the Global Leaderboard) — see Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Google Fonts — loads a web font file directly from Google's servers.
  • CrazyGames — if you're playing on crazygames.com, their own Privacy Policy and Terms of Service apply to that session, including any ads or SDK features their platform provides.

No accounts, no payments

The game never asks for an email address, a password, or payment information, and has no login of its own.
